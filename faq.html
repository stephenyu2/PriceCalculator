Frequently Asked Questions

Personalized instruction. Whether your child is struggling in one particular area, trying to get up to speed after a move, or wanting to supplement their current courses with more challenging materials, our instructors can create a program that is geared for your student's individual needs.

Homework support. There are times when a student's homework becomes too challenging. Our instructors can provide the answers your child needs, help to get the work done, and offer the tools for your child to work independently.

Increased confidence. This is an important factor for students in and out of the classroom. Tutoring can help a child feel more confident with their school work, which can boost self-esteem and help motivate them.

Increased comfort and participation levels. Many students do not speak up in class, and participation counts. Working with supportive teachers and asking and answering questions aloud will help them gain the comfort and confidence in a group setting.

Fewer interruptions. A classroom environment can have lots of distractions which can affect a student's comprehension, performance, and, ultimately, their self-esteem. This also applies to students who struggle with ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder), which is more common than many might think. With our teachers, students can focus on tasks with minimal distractions and fewer interruptions.

We most frequently tutor college, high school and middle school students but are capable of tutoring students anywhere along their academic journey.

We provide tutoring for a diverse range of learning levels:

  • Advanced students who want to get ahead and maximize their growth.
  • Students between letter grades who want to push themselves forward.
  • Students who need a private teacher to fill in educational gaps for a class.
  • Students who struggle with specific subjects and need one-on-one teaching to ensure they confidently grasp the material.

We tutor all school subjects including Math, Science, English, Humanities, and Language.

Our teachers are highly trained, experienced experts in their fields, having earned Bachelors, Masters, and/or PhD degrees. They have scored in the top 5% of the standardized tests they teach and are highly experienced with the curricula. They are able to help students not only master the subject material but also how to study independently.

We consider the student's academic needs, learning style, and personality traits to match them with a suitable tutor. We do our best to match your student with a tutor who we believe will be the perfect fit, and we check-in with students and tutors after the first session and will re-match students with a new tutor if necessary.

All tutoring sessions are conducted in person at our Northridge office located at 19849 Nordhoff Street, Northridge, California 91324. We do not offer online or at-home tutoring.
